# GTM.md — SpendLens Go-To-Market

## Exact target user

**Primary:** Engineering Manager at a Series A SaaS company (15–80 employees, $3M–$15M ARR), with 5–25 engineers. They were promoted from IC six to eighteen months ago. They own the engineering budget line in a spreadsheet their CFO reviews quarterly. They are not a buyer of AI tools — those purchases happened organically, one at a time, by individual engineers expensing subscriptions. They've just been handed a consolidated bill for the first time and it's larger than they expected.

**Secondary:** CTO at a seed-stage startup (2–10 engineers) where the CTO is also the one approving the Cursor and Claude subscriptions. They feel the spend but have no benchmark to know if it's reasonable.

## How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0 budget

**Week 1: Manual seeding (target: 20 users)**

Post a specific, data-rich thread on X: "I audited our AI tool spend for a 12-person eng team. Here's what we found: [actual numbers from a real audit]. Built a free tool so you can do this in 2 min: [link]." Tag 3–4 EMs who've publicly complained about tool costs. The thread must contain real numbers — not "significant savings" but "$340/month."

Post in Rands Leadership Slack #tools: "Built a free AI spend auditor for eng teams — no login, takes 2 min, shows you if you're on the wrong plan tier. Would love feedback from people who actually manage these budgets." Do NOT pitch Credex. This is a value-first post.

**Week 2: Hacker News Show HN (target: 40 users)**

"Show HN: Free AI tool spend auditor for eng teams — finds plan mismatches in 2 min." Post at 9am ET on a Tuesday. The title leads with the use case, not the brand. The comment thread should be ready with detailed answers about the audit logic — HN readers will probe the recommendation engine.

**Week 3: Newsletter seeding (target: 25 users)**

Cold email 10 newsletter authors who cover engineering tools (TLDR Engineering, Pointer.io, DevTools Weekly). Offer a brief + a pre-written blurb. Ask for a mention, not a feature. Subject line: "Free tool your readers who manage AI budgets might find useful — no pitch." Most won't respond; 2–3 mentions at this tier gets 20–30 users.

**Week 4: Community amplification (target: 15 users)**

Ask the first 40 users to share their audit result URL (the public share page) on X. Offer nothing — just ask. The share URL has OG preview that shows the savings number. A tweet that says "Just found out my team is overpaying $420/month for AI tools — link" gets clicks without any marketing copy.

## The unfair distribution channel

Credex already has relationships with the companies whose overforecast credits it sells — Anthropic, OpenAI, Cursor. These vendors have customer success teams who talk to their largest customers. A customer success rep at Anthropic who's about to have a renewal conversation with a $50k/year customer can forward SpendLens as a goodwill gesture: "before we talk renewal, here's a free audit to make sure you're on the right plan." That's a channel no competitor can replicate. Credex should activate this through its existing vendor relationships.
